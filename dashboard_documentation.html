About This Dashboard

This dashboard analyzes churn behavior, revenue exposure, and retention opportunities across 7,043 telecom customers. Use the tabs above to navigate from high-level KPIs through to an actionable list of at-risk customers.


Dataset: IBM Telco Customer Churn (public)  | Built with: PostgreSQL, Python, Tableau  | Last Updated: June 2026


Tab 1 — Overview

What you're looking at: Headline KPIs and a first look at where churn and revenue risk are concentrated.

How to read it:

  • The top row shows six key metrics for the full customer base
  • The bar chart shows churn rate by contract type -- month-to-month customers churn at 15x the rate of two-year customers
  • The donut chart on the right shows how MRR at risk breaks down by contract -- month-to-month contracts alone account for $120.8K of the $139.1K total

Start here if: You want the headline numbers before going deeper.


Tab 2 — Churn Analysis

What you're looking at: Churn rate broken down by four key dimensions, plus a cross-tab showing where contract type and internet service interact.

Filters available: Dependents, Partner status, Senior Citizen, Tenure Bucket

How to read it:

  • Each bar chart includes a reference line showing the overall average churn rate -- bars above the line are above average risk
  • The tenure bucket chart shows the trend line -- churn drops sharply after the first 12 months
  • The cross-tab at the bottom is color-coded: darker red = higher churn. The month-to-month + fiber optic cell at 57.9% is the highest-risk combination in the dataset

What to look for: Which customer profile you want to filter for, then check where their churn rate lands relative to the average.


Tab 3 — Revenue Analysis

What you're looking at: How monthly revenue is distributed across contract types and internet tiers, and where charges concentrate.

Filters available: Contract Type, Internet Tier

How to read it:

  • MRR bars are split by retained vs churned revenue -- red portions are already lost
  • The CLV chart shows estimated lifetime value by contract type. Two-year customers are worth significantly more over their lifetime
  • The scatter plot at the bottom shows revenue concentration -- most customers cluster at lower charge levels, with a small group of high-charge outliers worth monitoring

Note on CLV: Lifetime value figures are estimated as average monthly charges multiplied by average tenure. They are projections, not modeled values.


Tab 4 — Customer Segmentation

What you're looking at: The customer base divided into four risk tiers, with breakdowns by household type, demographics, and service count.

Filters available: Senior Segment, Gender, Contract Type

Risk tiers defined:

  • Churned -- already left (26.5%)
  • High Risk -- active, month-to-month, low tenure (13.8%)
  • Medium Risk -- active, some risk indicators (17.8%)
  • Low Risk -- active, long tenure or annual/two-year contract (41.9%)

What to look for: The add-on count chart is one of the clearest signals in the dataset. Churn drops from 37.7% at zero add-ons to 5.3% at six or more. Each service a customer subscribes to meaningfully reduces their likelihood of leaving.


Tab 5 — Services

What you're looking at: Churn rates for subscribers vs non-subscribers across all eight service types, plus service adoption and pricing by tier.

Filters available: Contract Type, Internet Service

How to read it:

  • For every service type, the red bar (non-subscribers) is taller than the blue bar (subscribers) -- this confirms that service adoption consistently reduces churn
  • The adoption rate chart shows which services customers already use most (phone service at 90.3%) and which have room to grow (tech support at 36.6%)
  • The avg. monthly charges chart at the bottom shows pricing by internet type and contract -- useful for understanding the revenue profile of each segment

What to look for: Services with low adoption rates and high churn differences between subscribers and non-subscribers are the strongest candidates for targeted upsell as a retention strategy.


Tab 6 — Explorer & Retention

What you're looking at: A prioritized list of high-risk customers who have not yet churned, with an estimate of how much revenue can be recovered through retention outreach.

Filters available: Contract Type, Internet Service, Senior Citizen, Risk Segment, Priority

How to read it:

  • The top KPI row summarizes the filtered group: 970 high-risk customers, 125 flagged as Priority 1, average tenure of 5 months, $47.8K MRR at risk
  • Priority 1 customers are ranked by monthly charge value -- highest-revenue customers at greatest risk get flagged first
  • The customer detail table lists each individual with their contract type, internet service, payment method, and charge data -- ready for a retention team to action
  • The bar chart on the right shows estimated monthly revenue recovered if these customers are retained

Important: The $47.8K recovery estimate assumes 100% retention success. It is an upper-bound illustration, not a forecast.

Use this tab if: You are building a retention outreach list or want to prioritize which customers to contact first.


Color Guide

  • Red — Churned customers, high churn rates, MRR at risk
  • Amber — High risk segments
  • Yellow — Retained customers, low churn rates, stable segments
  • Blue — Low risk or no-service segments


Tips for Navigation

• Filters apply within each tab only -- they reset when you switch tabs

• Hover over any chart element for a tooltip with exact values

• The color rule is consistent throughout: red = churn risk, blue = retained/stable, gold = medium risk
